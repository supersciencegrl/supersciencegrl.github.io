Study organic chemistry online

Here is a list of resources where you can learn and practise organic chemistry online, inspired by some friends who wanted practice for job interviews. Many thanks to Jacob Hiles for helping collect links.

Most are aimed at active researchers in the field, but some introductory resources are listed too. You can filter using the checkboxes at the top right.

I also have a Links page for other recommended chemistry/research resources.
It contains specific pages for:

  • HTE chemistry resources
  • Coding resources

Phil Baran lecture notes

Phil Baran – The Scripps Research Institute; advanced level

  • Heterocyclic chemistry

Jeff Bode open source lecture notes

Jeff Bode – ETH Zürich; advanced level
All quick references/study guides for the subject matter

  • Organic synthesis
  • FMO theory & pericyclic reactions
  • Simple protecting groups
  • Organometallics in synthesis
  • Catalysis in synthesis
  • Stereoselective reactions
  • Ligation & bioconjugation
  • Automated synthesis
  • Saturated N-heterocycle synthesis

Stu Cantrill lecture notes

Stu Cantrill – UCLA (now Springer Nature); introductory/advanced level

  • Introduction to organic chemistry
  • Introductory organic mechanisms
  • Retrosynthetic analysis
  • Nanoscience & novel materials

Dave Evans introduction to organic chemistry lecture notes

Dave Evans – Harvard University; introductory level
With appendices containing useful papers and pKa tables.

  • Introduction to organic chemistry
  • Pericyclic reactions
  • Simple mechanisms
  • Enol(ate) chemistry

University of York Exploring Everyday Chemistry lecture course

Cheminfographic

Roman Valiulin – Vertex Pharmaceuticals; introductory/advanced level
A collection of amazing infographics on many concepts in synthesis and medicinal chemistry. Thanks to Ed Sherer from Merck for pointing this out!

Chemistry by Design

Jon T Njardarson – University of Arizona; advanced/specialist level
See real, step-by-step total syntheses of thousands of molecules, with the option of quizzing yourself on products, reagents, and/or reaction names.

ACS DoC: Total Synthesis Database

Hans Reich – University of Wisconsin-Madison; advanced/specialist level
A database of total syntheses, filtered by named reactions.

SynArchive

Daniel Beaudoin & Philippe Murphy; advanced/specialist level
Browse a long list of total syntheses, by molecule name, date, PI, or carbon count.

Structure Elucidation Problems

Organic Structure Elucidation

Bradley Smith, Bill Boggess, & Jaroslav Zajicek – University of Notre Dame; advanced level
A workbook of structural determination problems, providing EI-MS, NMR and IR spectral data along with a molecular formula to elucidate the molecule. Answers are provided separately.

WebSpectra

Structural determination problems, providing NMR and IR spectral data along with a molecular formula to elucidate the molecule. Answers are provided separately.

CU Boulder Spectroscopy Problems

Nessa Carson: About

Nessa Carson is a professional synthetic organic chemist working with laboratory automation (robots) to perform high-throughput experimentation in the private sector in south east England.

To complement my organic chemistry experience, I like to get involved with a number of other science-related projects. A computer scientist friend told me that the best way to learn to code was to start by producing a personal website. I then wrote this website SuperScienceGrl without prior knowledge of CSS or JavaScript, and learned some Python with 1mwtt. The site's purpose is as a repository for content I think is useful, mostly to scientists. I keep a database of upcoming conferences in organic chemistry, and, for the Covid19 pandemic, a list of online chemistry events. For fun, you can also check out all the elements I've ever used in my time as a chemist (and make your own interactive checklist) here. I also write poetry.

Automated Intelligent Chemistry conference

Automation is a growing field of chemistry, in which I've been working closely since 2018. I'm co-organizing the first SCI Automated Intelligent Chemistry conference to bring together leading researchers in both industry and academia for an exciting day of talks, posters, and discussion.

To be held in Oct 2020

SCI Young Chemists' Panel

I am a committee member of the Society of Chemical Industry Young Chemists' Panel, where I'm organizing conferences and training events (including Automated Intelligent Chemistry) for pure and applied synthetic chemists.

Apr 2018–present

#UKChemJobs Twitterbot

Finding chemistry jobs in the UK is tough. I've created a Twitter bot that wants to help. It RTs any tweet containing the popular hashtag #ukchemjobs, and shares some original content links.
